The Instrument

The bark of soul. The purr of ballads.

The instrument behind this library is a Wurlitzer 200A: the classic American electric piano, built from the 1950s onward, that makes its sound with hammers striking steel reeds over an electrostatic pickup. A mechanism found in no other instrument: soft, it shimmers; hard, it barks.

That voice carried decades of soul, rock and pop. But the instrument is famously fragile: reeds snap mid-song, replacements must be tuned by hand with drops of solder, and the amplifiers hum with age. Every surviving unit is a little different, and slowly becoming rarer.

No two reed pianos age alike. This one is worth remembering.
