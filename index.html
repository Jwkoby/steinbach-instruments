Why We Do This

Instruments die quietly. Reeds snap, pipes fall silent, transistors drift, and the people who could repair them are becoming as rare as the instruments themselves. We record them before that happens: on location, register by register, key by key. Then we build them into playable instruments, so their voices outlive the hardware.

The Method

How an instrument survives.

01

On location

Recorded where the instrument lives: its room, its acoustics, its atmosphere included.

02

Voice by voice

Every register, key and switch position captured in isolation, at 24-bit / 96 kHz.

03

Faithfully mapped

Built into KONTAKT and standalone instruments with a GUI that mirrors the original panel.

04

Released & preserved

Published as a permanent archive, for artists, producers and researchers.

Behind The Project

Two people. One archive.

Steinbach Instruments is a two-person project: an audio engineer and a 3D artist, recording endangered instruments and turning them into software, carefully, one instrument at a time.
