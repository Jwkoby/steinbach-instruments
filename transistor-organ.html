The Instrument

From Dutch living rooms. To the records.

The instrument behind this library is a Philips Philicorda: launched in the early 1960s as one of the first electronic organs built for the home, an instrument for the family, sold next to radios and record players. Slender steel legs, rocker tabs, and a tone somewhere between reed organ and space age.

What was designed as furniture became a cult sound: garage bands, lounge productions and indie records discovered its honest, slightly eccentric voice. Yet surviving units are fading: aging transistors, drifting circuits, and no factory left to repair them.

Built for living rooms. Remembered for records.
